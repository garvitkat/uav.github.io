Agastya UAV

Agastya was our first attempt at carbon fibre manufacturing. The aircraft was made of semi monocoque composite construction and was capable of fully autonomous flight. Designed for max take-off weight of 18kgs, it has been tested for autonomous flight for 3000ft altitude with and endurance of 35 minutes.


Amphibian UAV

This aircraft was made in collaboration with Indian Maritime foundation as an amphibious aircraft for surveillance of coast lines and fish schools. Manufactured with a semi monocoque construction, it has been tested up to heights of 3000ft with an endurance of 55 minutes, Along autonomous flight and take-off.


Man-Portable Aerial Surveillance System (MPASS)

MPASS was design as a low cost and expendable UAV, which could be carried by one man and setup under one minute. It has the capability of folding wings, folding tail and a collapsible boom which makes it very compact and lets us carry it in a backpack. The prototype weighed in at a mere 1.6kgs, having time of 20 minutes with autonomous flight.

About Us

Team UAV is a team of aviation enthusiasts under Creation Labs, VIT University, Vellore. We design and fabricate Medium range UAVs capable of performing surveillance and target acquisition with the fully autonomous waypoint navigation along with remote sensing and GIS. The main objective of the team is to undertake projects which have myriad real life applications along with outstanding potential for research in plethora of fields. The two main departments of the team are Electrical and Mechanical, which are further bifurcated into smaller departments based on specialization like design, structure, CAD, Image processing, mathematical modelling.

Our Departments


Design

The department is responsible for the design of an aircraft which includes aerodynamic calculations, control and stability, aesthetics, CAD modelling and CFD simulations.

Avionics

All the work related to control of aircraft in the air, communication with the aircraft, operation of electronic payloads and processing of data is handled by the avionics department.

Structure

Structures department is responsible for the structural design and analysis of the aircraft, decisions regarding manufacturing processes and fabrication work.
